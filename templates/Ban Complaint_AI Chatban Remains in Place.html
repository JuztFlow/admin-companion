How does the automatic system work?
  • When another player uses the in-game report function on one of your messages, the system automatically analyzes the complete chat logs from the relevant conversation and corresponding chat (all chat, normal chat, whisper chat, group chat, guild chat, etc.) within the last 30 minutes of the reported message.
  • To ensure full context, the analyzed chatlog contains not only your messages but also those of every other player who spoke during that period. This allows the system to account for the circumstances in which your potentially toxic message was sent and to consider any mitigating factors.
  • Before analysis begins, the entire chatlog is fully anonymized. This prevents any bias based on who wrote the messages. The system evaluates only what was said, not who said it or whether that person has prior violations.
  • The analysis produces a numerical toxicity score assigned to you for that specific report. If the system finds no wrongdoing, the score will be 0, effectively voiding the report.
  • Your overall toxicity score accumulates over time. Each non-zero score from an individual report remains active for 30 days, after which it expires and is removed from your total. If your accumulated score ever exceeds a certain threshold, the system automatically issues a penalty, either an account-wide chat ban or a battlemode chat ban, depending on where the most recent violation took place. The duration of the penalty is determined solely by your previous infractions (as outlined in rule 3.9), not by the severity of the latest violation.
  • If you have been chat-banned within the last 30 days, the threshold for triggering a new penalty is temporarily halved. This is intended to discourage repeat offenses by making subsequent penalties easier to trigger.
  • The toxicity threshold needed to trigger a penalty is subject to change and will not be publicly communicated for now until we have finetuned the system to the point where we believe it to be final.
